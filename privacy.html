3. Information We Collect

Account and business information

  • Name, username, company name, role, email address, telephone number, and contact details;
  • Account credentials, authentication records, preferences, and settings;
  • Billing profile, wallet, invoice, transaction, refund, credit, payout, and payment-related records;
  • Business verification, tax, compliance, and identity information where required.

Advertiser and campaign information

  • Campaign names, offers, targeting, country tiers, niches, budgets, lead requirements, and cost-per-lead bids;
  • Funnel pages, form fields, creative materials, URLs, tracking parameters, and campaign rules;
  • Lead acceptance, rejection, verification, duplicate, quality, fraud, and conversion records;
  • Autoresponder API and webhook configuration details supplied by advertisers.

Publisher and traffic information

  • Publisher profile, source, placement, referral, payout, and campaign information;
  • Clicks, impressions, referrals, submissions, conversions, timestamps, and attribution records;
  • Traffic-quality, compliance, device, browser, fraud, and performance signals.

Lead and opt-in information

The information collected through a campaign depends on the fields configured by the advertiser and may include:

  • Name, email address, telephone number, country, region, city, or postal code;
  • Answers to campaign-specific or qualification questions;
  • Consent records, date and time, source URL, IP address, and tracking information;
  • Browser, device, referral, campaign, verification, duplicate, fraud, and lead-quality information.

Technical and usage information

  • IP address, device identifiers, browser, operating system, language, and approximate location;
  • Login history, activity logs, pages visited, clicks, sessions, referring URLs, and errors;
  • Cookie identifiers, analytics data, platform performance, diagnostics, and security events.

Support and communications

We collect information contained in support tickets, emails, feedback, demonstrations, onboarding, surveys, and other communications.

Sensitive information

Leadvix is not designed for collecting special-category or highly sensitive personal information unless specifically approved in writing. Users must not collect health, biometric, government identification, precise financial, racial or ethnic, religious, political, sexual-orientation, or similar sensitive information without a lawful basis, required disclosures and consent, and our written approval.

We may receive information directly from you; from advertisers, publishers, affiliates, funnel owners, and campaign partners; from individuals submitting campaign forms; from connected autoresponders, webhooks, payment providers, and analytics tools; automatically from devices, cookies, logs, and security systems; and from fraud prevention, verification, compliance, or public sources where legally permitted.

  • Capture, verify, score, route, and deliver lead opt-ins;

  • Detect bots, duplicate submissions, suspicious activity, abuse, invalid data, and fraud;
  • Calculate campaign charges, credits, refunds, publisher earnings, adjustments, and payouts;
  • Provide analytics, reporting, attribution, AI-assisted lead scoring, and campaign optimization;
  • Provide done-for-you campaign optimization and support;
  • Send account, campaign, transaction, security, service, and support communications;
  • Improve platform functionality, reliability, usability, and product development;
  • Enforce agreements, policies, quality standards, and legal rights;
  • Meet tax, accounting, regulatory, legal, and dispute-resolution obligations;
  • Send marketing communications where legally permitted and subject to opt-out rights.
Lead verification confirms that a submission passed the applicable validation and quality checks. It does not guarantee that a lead will purchase, respond, or produce any particular commercial result.

Advertisers and publishers must provide clear and legally compliant disclosures on funnels and forms. They are responsible for:

We may use cookies, pixels, local storage, scripts, and similar technologies for login and authentication, security, preferences, fraud prevention, analytics, campaign attribution, conversion measurement, and advertising where permitted.

  • Strictly necessary: authentication, sessions, security, and core platform operation;

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, loss, misuse, and destruction. Measures may include access controls, encryption, monitoring, logs, backups, vendor reviews, and secure development practices.

No system is completely secure. Users are responsible for protecting credentials, securing API keys and webhook destinations, limiting account access, and promptly reporting suspected unauthorized activity.
